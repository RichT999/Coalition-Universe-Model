# Impedance vs Density Math ($Z:\rho$)

This framework defines the medium-based wave mechanics of the Coalition Model.

### 1. The Characteristic Constant
The medium's impedance ($Z$) to volumetric mass density ($\rho$) ratio is defined as:
$$\xi = \frac{Z}{\rho}$$

### 2. Velocity Constraints
Wave propagation velocity ($v$) is limited by the medium's elasticity ($\epsilon$) and the $Z:\rho$ constant:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{1}{\xi \cdot \epsilon}}$$

### 3. Singularity Boundary (The Trinca Maximum)
At the "Black Hole Turn-Off," mass density reaches its absolute invariant limit:
$$\rho_{max} \approx 1.02 \times 10^{18} \text{ kg/m}^3$$
